Dark Canvas is a tool for making generative art in the browser. Pick an algorithm, dial in the parameters, hit generate, and export — all on a black canvas.

## Algorithms Included

- **Flow Field** — particles follow a Perlin noise vector field
- **Voronoi** — colored cell diagrams with adjustable seed count
- **L-System** — fractal plant/tree structures from grammar rules
- **Recursive Subdivisions** — Mondrian-style geometric layouts
- **Circle Packing** — fills a space with non-overlapping circles
- **Strange Attractors** — Lorenz, Clifford, and De Jong

## What You Can Control

Each algorithm exposes its own set of sliders and toggles: color palette, iteration count, line weight, randomness seed, and canvas dimensions. Changing the seed regenerates the output while keeping all other settings.

## Export

Export as a PNG at up to 4× the screen resolution — suitable for printing or use as a wallpaper. The canvas dimensions and DPI multiplier are configurable before export.

## Why Dark Canvas

I wanted a place to experiment with generative algorithms without writing boilerplate every time. Dark Canvas is that scratchpad — built once, used constantly.
